6  Skeletal Terms, Bone Histology, and Microanatomy

6.1 Prelab Activity 6.1

6.1.1 Skeleton

  • Axial – the portion of the skeleton forming the central axis of the body; includes the skull, vertebral column, and thoracic cage
  • Appendicular skeleton – the portion of the skeleton consisting of the limbs and the girdles that attach them to the axial skeleton

6.1.2 General Structure of Long Bones

  • Epiphysis – enlarged ends of a long bone containing spongy bone and red marrow
  • Diaphysis – shaft of a long bone composed mainly of compact bone
  • Compact bone – dense bone tissue forming the outer layer of bones
  • Spongy bone – porous bone tissue composed of trabeculae; found at bone ends
  • Hyaline (articular) cartilage – smooth cartilage covering the epiphyses; reduces friction
  • Periosteum – fibrous membrane covering the outer surface of bone
  • Bone processes – projections where muscles and ligaments attach
  • Medullary cavity – hollow cavity in the diaphysis containing marrow
  • Yellow marrow – fat-storing tissue in adult bones
  • Red marrow – tissue where blood cell formation occurs
  • Epiphyseal plate – cartilage growth plate in immature bones
  • Endosteum (simple squamous) – inner bone lining covering marrow cavity

6.1.3 Bone Tissue

Term Definition
Osteocytes Mature bone cells that maintain bone tissue
Matrix Extracellular substance made of collagen and minerals
Collagen fibers Provide tensile strength
Calcium phosphate Mineral salts that harden bone
Osteoblasts Bone-forming cells
Osteoclasts Bone-resorbing cells

6.1.4 Bone Classification

Term Definition
Long bones Bones longer than wide; act as levers
Short bones Cube-shaped bones providing stability
Flat bones Thin, flat bones that protect organs
Irregular bones Bones with complex shapes
Sesamoid bones Bones embedded in tendons

6.5 Lab Activity 6.3

Major differences between compact and spongy bone:

  • Compact bone is dense and organized into osteons
  • Spongy bone is porous, arranged in trabeculae, and contains marrow spaces
