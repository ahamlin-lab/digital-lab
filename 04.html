4  Histology/Tissues

4.1 Prelab Activity 4.1

4.1.1 Types of tissues

  • Epithelial – tissue that covers surfaces, lines cavities, and forms glands; functions in protection, absorption, filtration, and secretion
  • Connective – tissue that supports, binds, and protects other tissues; includes bone, cartilage, blood, and fat
  • Muscular – tissue that contracts to produce movement
  • Nervous – tissue that receives, processes, and transmits electrical signals

4.1.2 Epithelial Tissues

  • Simple squamous – a single layer of flattened cells specialized for diffusion and filtration
  • Stratified squamous (keratinized/nonkeratinized) – multiple layers of flattened cells that protect against abrasion; keratinized forms the epidermis, nonkeratinized lines moist cavities
  • Simple cuboidal – a single layer of cube-shaped cells for secretion and absorption
  • Stratified cuboidal/columnar – rare tissues with multiple layers that provide protection and secretion
  • Simple columnar – a single layer of tall cells for absorption and secretion
  • Pseudostratified columnar epithelium – appears multilayered but is a single layer; often ciliated and contains goblet cells
  • Transitional epithelium – stratified tissue that stretches and allows distension of urinary organs

4.1.3 Epithelial Features

4.1.3.1 Epithelial layers

  • Simple – one layer of cells
  • Stratified – two or more layers of cells

4.1.3.2 Surfaces

  • Apical – free surface exposed to a lumen or external environment
  • Basal – surface attached to the basement membrane
  • Basement membrane – thin layer anchoring epithelium to underlying connective tissue

4.1.4 Connective Tissue

  • Cartilage – flexible connective tissue providing support and cushioning
  • Areolar connective tissue – loose connective tissue that wraps and cushions organs
  • Hyaline cartilage – smooth cartilage providing support and flexibility
  • Adipose tissue – connective tissue storing fat and providing insulation
  • Fibrocartilage – tough cartilage providing tensile strength
  • Elastic cartilage – flexible cartilage maintaining shape
  • Regular dense connective tissue – parallel collagen fibers providing strong attachment
  • Irregular dense connective tissue – irregular collagen fibers resisting stress in multiple directions
  • Bone – hard connective tissue providing support and mineral storage
  • Blood – fluid connective tissue that transports gases, nutrients, and wastes

4.1.5 Connective Tissue Features

  • Collagen fibers – strong fibers providing tensile strength
  • Elastin fibers – stretchy fibers allowing recoil
  • Extracellular matrix – material between cells providing support
  • Cells – the living components of connective tissue
    • Fibroblasts – cells that produce fibers and matrix
    • Adipocytes – fat-storing cells
    • Chondrocytes – cartilage cells
  • Lacuna – cavity where bone or cartilage cells reside
