4.1 Objectives

During this lab you will use the microscope and diagrams to review the four tissue types as well as familiarize yourself with their location and function in the body.

Upon completion of this lab session, you should be able to:

  • Name a tissue as epithelial, connective, muscular, or nervous.
  • Explain how tissue structure supports function.
  • Describe structure, function, and location of tissues.
  • Identify epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissues on slides.

4.2 Prelab Activity 4.1

4.2.1 Types of Tissues

  • Epithelial – tissue that covers surfaces, lines cavities, and forms glands; functions in protection, absorption, filtration, and secretion
  • Connective – tissue that supports, binds, and connects other tissues
  • Muscular – tissue that contracts to produce movement
  • Nervous – tissue that receives and transmits electrical impulses

4.2.2 Epithelial Tissues

  • Simple squamous – single layer of flat cells for diffusion and filtration
  • Stratified squamous (keratinized/nonkeratinized) – multiple layers for protection
  • Simple cuboidal – secretion and absorption
  • Stratified cuboidal/columnar – rare protective tissues
  • Simple columnar – absorption and secretion
  • Pseudostratified columnar epithelium – ciliated; mucus movement
  • Transitional epithelium – stretchy epithelium of urinary organs

4.2.3 Epithelial Features

Layers - Simple – one layer
- Stratified – multiple layers

Surfaces - Apical – free surface
- Basal – attached surface
- Basement membrane – anchors epithelium to connective tissue

4.2.4 Connective Tissue

  • Cartilage – flexible support
  • Areolar connective tissue – wraps and cushions
  • Hyaline cartilage – smooth support
  • Adipose tissue – fat storage, insulation
  • Fibrocartilage – tensile strength
  • Elastic cartilage – flexible support
  • Dense regular CT – parallel collagen fibers
  • Dense irregular CT – multidirectional strength
  • Bone – support and mineral storage
  • Blood – transport medium

4.2.5 Connective Tissue Features

  • Collagen fibers – tensile strength
  • Elastin fibers – elasticity
  • Extracellular matrix – support and medium
  • Fibroblasts – fiber production
  • Adipocytes – fat storage
  • Chondrocytes – cartilage cells
  • Lacuna – cavity housing cells
